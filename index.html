Hi, I'm Aarav Pillai!

I work on creative AI and develop ML systems, with a particular interest in how generative technologies are designed, governed, and quietly permitted to make decisions about culture and people. I am less taken with technological spectacle than with the practical afterlife of these systems; what they assume, what they normalise, and how policy language behaves once it encounters deployed code.

I currently lead research at Qinārā AI Lab, and have previously worked as an associate lecturer at the UAL Creative Computing Institute, while also working as an independent data scientist. I am now preparing for doctoral research in Computer Science, where my focus is creative AI and technology governance.

MSc Thesis

thesis focus: timbre transfer, autistic stimming, and assistive sound design

My master’s thesis examined how timbre-transfer models might be repurposed not for musical novelty, but for self-regulation; specifically to support autistic stimming practices rather than suppress them.

The project sits at the intersection of Creative AI, accessibility, and care; it treats sound not as output, but as infrastructure.
