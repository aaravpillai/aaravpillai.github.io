hello, i'm aarav pillai (he/him)

i work on creative ai and develop ml systems, with a particular interest in how generative technologies are designed, governed, and quietly permitted to make decisions about culture and people. i am less taken with technological spectacle than with the practical afterlife of these systems; what they assume, what they normalise, and how policy language behaves once it encounters deployed code.

i currently lead research at qinārā ai lab, and have previously worked as an associate lecturer at the ual creative computing institute, where i delivered lectures, graded a small mountain of assignments, and guided students to navigate the delightful intersection of ethical computation and creativity across the msc data science & ai for the creative industries + ma/msc computing & creative industry programmes. i also worked as an independent data scientist for a year, working on government and humanitarian projects. i am now preparing for doctoral research in computer science, where my focus is creative ai and technology governance.

msc dissertation

dissertation focus: timbre transfer, autistic stimming, and assistive sound design

my dissertation explored how timbre-transfer models might be repurposed not for musical novelty, but for self-regulation; specifically to support autistic stimming practices rather than suppress them.

the project sits at the intersection of creative ai, accessibility, and care; it treats sound not as output, but as infrastructure.
