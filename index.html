hello, i'm aarav pillai (he/him)

i work on creative ml + healthcare ai, with a particular interest in how generative technologies are designed, governed, and quietly permitted to make decisions about culture and people. i am less taken with technological spectacle than with the practical afterlife of these systems; what they assume, what they normalise, and how policy language behaves once it encounters deployed code.

i currently lead research at qinārā ai lab, and previously worked as an associate lecturer at the ual creative computing institute; there, i delivered lectures, graded a small mountain of assignments, and guided students to navigate the delightful intersection of ethical computation and creativity across the msc data science & ai for the creative industries + ma/msc computing & creative industry programmes. i also spent a year as an independent data scientist, working across government and humanitarian projects. i am now preparing for doctoral research in computer science, a stage characterised by provisional plans, overannotated pdfs, and an unreasonable number of open tabs.
